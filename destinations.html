Amazing Destinations

Explore the places we've visited and the cultures we've discovered.

Barcelona, Spain

Barcelona is a city known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant culture. We had the chance to visit the iconic Sagrada Familia, the famous Park Güell, and walked along the vibrant La Rambla street. Each corner of the city has a story to tell!

Tourist Route: Start at the Sagrada Familia, then head to the Gothic Quarter to explore the narrow streets. Don’t miss the beautiful beaches and finish your day with tapas at a local restaurant.

Fun Fact: Barcelona is the birthplace of modernist architecture, and Gaudí's works are a UNESCO World Heritage Site!

Secret Spot: After the main tourist sites, head to the **Bunkers del Carmel** for a hidden gem. This old military site offers panoramic views of the entire city and is less crowded than other spots, perfect for a peaceful sunset.

Kyoto, Japan

Kyoto is a city steeped in tradition, famous for its ancient temples, beautiful gardens, and stunning Geisha culture. We explored the famous Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), experienced a traditional tea ceremony, and wandered through the beautiful Arashiyama Bamboo Grove.

Tourist Route: Start at the Fushimi Inari Shrine with its thousands of red torii gates, then head to the Golden Pavilion. Don’t forget to visit the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove and the Kiyomizu-dera temple for breathtaking views.

Fun Fact: Kyoto has over 1,600 Buddhist temples, making it the cultural heart of Japan!

Secret Spot: Skip the busy Gion district and visit the **Kifune Shrine** in the outskirts of Kyoto. It's a peaceful place surrounded by lush forests and famous for its beautiful lantern-lit paths in the evening.

Oaxaca, Mexico

Oaxaca is a cultural gem in Mexico, known for its indigenous heritage, delicious food, and vibrant arts scene. We visited the stunning Monte Albán archaeological site and explored the colorful markets filled with handmade crafts and fresh produce. The food in Oaxaca is some of the best we’ve ever tasted!

Tourist Route: Start with a visit to Monte Albán, an ancient Zapotec city. Next, head to the Zócalo (main square) and try a traditional Oaxacan meal like mole, then visit the local artisan shops.

Fun Fact: Oaxaca is the birthplace of Mezcal, a famous Mexican spirit made from agave. It's definitely worth trying while you're there!

Secret Spot: For a more intimate experience, visit the **Hierve el Agua**, a set of petrified waterfalls in the mountains near Oaxaca. The stunning landscape and natural mineral springs offer an off-the-beaten-path escape.
